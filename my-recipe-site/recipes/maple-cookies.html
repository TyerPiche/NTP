Ingredients

  • 3 cups (360 g.) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1 cup raw walnuts
  • 1 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup (200 g.) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tbsp. whole milk
  • 1 tsp. pure maple syrup
  • 3/4 c. (160 g.) light brown sugar
  • 6 tbsp. unsalted butter
  • 1/2 tsp. maple extract or pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp. pure maple syrup
  • 3/4 c. (160 g.) light brown sugar
  • 6 tbsp. unsalted butter
  • 1/2 tsp. maple extract or pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp. pure maple syrup
  • 11/2 c. (175 g.) confectioners' sugar
  • Pinch of kosher salt
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg
  • 3 tbsp. whole milk
  • Crushed raw walnuts and maple sugar, for serving (optional) A maple leaf-shaped cookie cutter

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, pulse flour and walnuts until walnuts are finely ground. Add baking powder, cinnamon, and salt and pulse until combined.
  2. In a large bowl, using a handheld mixer on medium-high speed, beat butter and granulated sugar until fluffy and pale in color, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add egg, milk, and syrup and beat until combined.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients and beat on medium-low speed until combined and no dry streaks remain.
  5. Transfer dough to a clean surface and wrap with plastic wrap. Refrigerate at least 1 hour or up to 2 days.
  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll dough to 1/8" thick. Cut cookies into maple shapes, re-rolling scraps if necessary, for a total of about 48.
  7. Transfer to 2 parchment-lined baking sheets. Freeze until cold, about 10 minutes.
  8. Arrange racks in upper and lower thirds of oven; preheat to 350°.
  9. Bake cookies, rotating sheets top to bottom halfway through, until edges start to turn lightly golden, 10 to 12 minutes. Let cool.
  10. In a small saucepan over low heat, cook brown sugar and butter, stirring constantly to avoid burning, until sugar is dissolved, 2 to 3 minutes.
  11. Transfer to a heatproof medium bowl and let cool slightly. Whisk in maple extract and maple syrup. Slowly add confectioners' sugar until combined, then stir in salt and nutmeg.
  12. Whisk in milk; the icing will be light brown.
  13. Dip one side of each cookie into icing and let excess drip off.
  14. Decorate with crushed walnuts or maple sugar (if using).
